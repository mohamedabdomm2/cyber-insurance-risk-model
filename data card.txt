This dataset contains 10,000 synthetic, highly realistic business records designed to train an underwriting model for a Cyber Insurance provider.
The data maps a company's firmographics, cybersecurity defensive posture, and data exposure to their historical likelihood 
of suffering a catastrophic cyber breach and filing an insurance claim.


Part 1: The Identity Layer (Firmographics)
These features define the "size and shape" of the target.

company_id (Int): Primary Key. Unique identifier for the client.
industry_sector (Categorical): The company's vertical (e.g., Healthcare, Finance, Retail, Technology). Note: Healthcare & Finance are historically targeted more frequently.
employee_count (Int): Total number of staff (represents the number of "phish-able" targets).
annual_revenue (Float): Total yearly revenue in USD. Used to calculate potential Business Interruption losses.
operating_budget (Float): Yearly operating expenditure. 
years_in_operation (Int): Company maturity. Older companies may have more insecure "legacy" systems.
hq_location (Categorical): Country of headquarters, which dictates data privacy law jurisdiction (e.g., GDPR, CCPA).

Part 2: The Defensive Layer (Security Posture)
These are the Risk Mitigators. Stronger defenses should correlate negatively with claim probability.

mfa_status (Binary: 0/1): Is Multi-Factor Authentication enforced across all internal systems?
edr_deployed (Binary: 0/1): Does the company use Endpoint Detection & Response (EDR) software?
patch_velocity (Int): Average number of days it takes the IT team to patch a "Critical" vulnerability.
open_vulnerabilities (Int): Current number of unpatched flaws.
backup_type (Categorical): How data is backed up (Offline/Air-gapped, Cloud-only, On-premise).
training_freq (Int): How often employees get security awareness training (0 = Never, 4 = Quarterly).
pen_test_age (Int): Months since the last professional 3rd-party "Hacker" test was conducted.
Part 3: The Crown Jewels Layer (Data Exposure)
These features define the severity and attractiveness of the target to hackers.

pii_count (Int): Total count of Personally Identifiable Information records stored by the company.
database_storage_tb (Float): Total data footprint in Terabytes. 
pci_data_flag (Binary: 0/1): Does the company process Credit Card information?
phi_data_flag (Binary: 0/1): Does the company process Protected Health Information?
cloud_adoption_pct (Float): Percentage of infrastructure hosted in the Cloud (AWS/Azure) vs. On-premise.
subdomain_count (Int): Number of public-facing web addresses (defines the external "Attack Surface").
Part 4: The Ground Truth (Incident History & The Target)
This data represents the aftermath. For non-breached companies (y=0), these fields (except the target) are NaN.

is_claim_filed (Binary: 0/1): TARGET VARIABLE. Did the client suffer a breach and use their insurance?
breach_type (Categorical): The vector of attack (Ransomware, Phishing, DDoS, Insider Threat).
total_loss_val (Float): The total financial cost of the claim.
records_stolen (Float): Actual number of records leaked in the event.
forensics_retained (Float: 1.0 or NaN): Did the company hire a digital forensics team post-breach?
post_breach_pr_cost (Float): Money spent on Public Relations to save the brand image after a hack.
